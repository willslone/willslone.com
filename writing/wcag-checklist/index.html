WCAG Checklist

There’s no shortage of WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) checklists on the web, but since none of them checked all my boxes, I built my own.

In this post

  1. What it does
  2. Who it’s for
  3. Where to find it

What it does

The checklist covers all of WCAG 2.2. You can filter by conformance level — A, AA, or AAA — so you’re only looking at what’s relevant to your audit. As you work through each criterion, check it off. You can also toggle a view that hides everything you’ve already checked, leaving only what’s left to test.

Each criterion includes a link to its official Understanding document for quick reference.

Your progress saves automatically in the browser, so you can close the tab and pick up where you left off. And since the whole thing lives in a single file, you can download it and use it offline if you prefer.

Who it’s for

Checklists are useful at any experience level. Even seasoned auditors can overlook something. That said, it’s particularly handy for people newer to auditing who are still building familiarity with the full set of success criteria.

Where to find it

You can use it online or download it for offline use.
